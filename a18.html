ANXIETY UNMASKED: SHEDDING LIGHT ON THE SILENT STRUGGLE

Imagine waking up in the morning, feeling the weight of the day ahead pressing down on your chest like a boulder. Your mind races with million of thoughts , each one more overwhelming than the last. You try to get out of the bed but your body feels like it’s encased in cement ,immobilized by the fear of facing the day. This is just one moment in the life of someone grappling with anxiety , where even the simplest task can feel like insurmountable obstacles or picture yourself standing in a crowded room surrounded by people chatting and laughing. despite the noise and activity ,you feel isolated in your own bubble of worry. Your heart races , palm sweat and every breath feels shallow as if the air is too thick to inhale. You try to join the conversation ,but the fear of saying the wrong thing or being judged paralyzes you. You plaster on a smile, hoping no one notices the turmoil churning inside you. This is the invisible battle of anxiety, where every social interaction becomes a battlefield of self doubt and apprehension.

In my own journey with anxiety , I’ve often find myself in situations similar to the ones described above. The most recent one was during one of our tests, I was terribly anxious before the test , things changed for the worse during the test, My palms were sweaty and started spasming ,even holding the pen became difficult ,consequently my hands were shivering and ultimately I couldn’t concentrate in the paper well and messed up with the calculations. Finally ended up at home weeping due to my inability to reproduce my knowledge properly during the test. Apart from this I’ve experienced suffocating grip of social anxiety, where every heartbeat feels deafeningly loud ,and every glance in my direction feels like a spotlight highlighting my insecurities .these personal experiences have given me a firsthand understanding of the debilitating effect of anxiety. To my dismay , I’ve never had someone understanding my condition in all these years ,never did anyone lend a helping hand to make me feel better. Instead I was often accused to be overreacting and exaggerating my situation , which would further worsen my condition. It’s a pity that in a country where around 7.3% of population experience anxiety disorders, Mental health disorders are still not taken as seriously as other physical disorders and seeking medical help for the same is still considered as taboo.

In our society, the insensitivity towards individuals struggling with anxiety can be particularly pronounced due to societal stigmas surrounding mental health. Many people may dismiss anxiety as a character flaw or a sign of weakness, rather than recognizing it as a legitimate medical condition. Instead of offering support and empathy, individuals with anxiety are often met with judgment, criticism, or even ridicule. In social settings, there's often a lack of awareness about the nuances of anxiety disorders, leading to insensitive comments or actions that further exacerbate the suffering of those affected. Phrases like "just relax" or "stop worrying so much" trivialize the complex nature of anxiety and invalidate the experiences of those struggling with it. Moreover, the expectation of always appearing strong and composed in Indian culture can add an additional layer of pressure on individuals with anxiety, making it even more difficult for them to seek help or speak openly about their struggles.

Overall, anxiety is a pervasive and often misunderstood mental health issue that affects millions of individuals worldwide, including a significant portion of the population in India. Despite its prevalence, the insensitivity and stigma surrounding anxiety persist, creating barriers to understanding and support. It's imperative for society to recognize the debilitating impact of anxiety and work towards fostering a culture of empathy, acceptance, and support for those affected. By raising awareness, promoting open dialogue, and prioritizing mental health education and resources, we can create a more inclusive and compassionate environment where individuals struggling with anxiety feel heard, understood, and empowered to seek the help they deserve. Together, we can break the silence surrounding anxiety and pave the way towards a healthier, more supportive future for all.

-PRADEEPTA KAR
