The tree that stands out

The tree that stands out.
Thousands of visitors visit the world heritage site of the Ellora caves and the spectacular Kailash Temple which is a testament to the marvelous architecture that dates back to the 6th century AD.

The caves are an astounding example of human intelligence and its understanding of the concepts of architecture, design and precision. This region is also blessed with impressive natural beauty. The caves are surrounded by trees and hills which are not of course as ancient as or as attractive as the caves but need to be admired, not just at this place but at every place of importance ‘for who can be a better creator than the lord almighty, who created us all. ’There are two things that I look forward to when a trip to the caves is planned; the first is the waterfall that runs perpendicular through the cave line falling from a height of over 100 meters (only in monsoons though!) , and second the primary subject of this article is the perennial “TREE”.

At the entrance of the Kailash Temple at Ellora on your right when you face the temple is this tree which has its tentacles spread out (if you have seen Harry Potter you may remember the Whomping Willow) furthermore it feels as if it is looking at us, wavering its form leaf-less sometimes, full of leaves sometimes.

The tree can be seen as a testament to the concept of one-of-a-kind, in its wholeness it has a distinct charm to it with its perfectly tree like shape that we are used to drawing in the monsoons to the way in which it stands out through the extremely dry season of summer. Also to add an observation, the circular pavement around the tree is covered with grass all year round and surprisingly even when the tree is barren of leaves and the surroundings are brown in summers, the grass on the pavement is still green! This picture taken in the month of March 2024 justifies the same.

What can be perceived here is that the tree in its own being, its roots deep around the rocks, foundations of the magnificent Kailash Temple, its leaves and branches surrounded by the lush gardens maintained by the ASI; but the tree is in sync with the nature while refusing to blend in its surrounding it makes a statement, a statement that no matter how great your peers, your neighbors and your environment what matters most is ‘you’ because you are an inseparable part of the universe, a one of its kind creation and a single entity in the equation of the universe. The tree reminds us to bloom, grow, prosper in a way that suits us, in a way that we are comfortable with, in a way that makes us happy. It shows us how to stand out without standing alone, it can also be seen as humbled by the presence of the Kailash Temple and humbleness goes a long way! The caves are immortal in most senses, they have existed for centuries and they will exist for centuries to come; the tree a mortal being with its limited years is fulfilling its karma to stand tall through the days rainy and sunny, through the weekends and weekdays greeting visitors from worldwide at this World Heritage Site standing tall and confident in its oneness.

- Rahi Gaikwad
